Privacy Policy

OnePlayer for Safari · Last updated April 20, 2026

Summary: OnePlayer does not collect, store, or transmit any user data. Everything stays on your Mac.

What OnePlayer does

OnePlayer is a Safari Web Extension for YouTube. It runs entirely in your browser and provides three features: automatic pause of background YouTube tabs, sticky Picture-in-Picture when you scroll past the player, and a manual PiP button on the YouTube video player.

Data collection

OnePlayer does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data, usage data, or content. No account is required. No information ever leaves your device through OnePlayer.

Local storage

OnePlayer uses Safari Extension Storage (browser.storage.local) to remember your preferences on your device only. The values stored are:

  • Whether Auto-Pause is enabled
  • Whether Sticky Picture-in-Picture is enabled
  • Whether the manual PiP button is shown on the player

These settings never leave your Mac and are not synced, uploaded, or shared.

Tracking and analytics

No analytics or tracking scripts are used. OnePlayer contains no third-party SDKs, no telemetry, and no advertising frameworks.

Permissions

OnePlayer requests the following Safari permissions strictly to provide its features:

  • Access to youtube.com — to detect the video player and control playback
  • Tabs and scripting — to coordinate pausing across open YouTube tabs
  • Storage — to save your preferences locally

Children's privacy

OnePlayer is suitable for all ages and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.
