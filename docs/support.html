Getting started

  1. Open Safari → Settings → Extensions.
  2. Enable OnePlayer.
  3. Click OnePlayer's icon in the Safari toolbar and allow access to youtube.com.
  4. Visit any YouTube video — OnePlayer works automatically.

Frequently asked questions

Auto-Pause isn't pausing other tabs

Make sure OnePlayer has access to youtube.com on every tab (Safari toolbar icon → Always Allow on Every Website). Reload any tabs that were open before you granted permission.

Sticky Picture-in-Picture doesn't trigger when I scroll

PiP activates when you scroll past roughly 75% of the player. Safari also requires a recent user interaction with the page before PiP can be entered — try clicking the video once and scrolling again. You can also use the manual PiP button on the player.

I don't see the PiP button on the player

Open the OnePlayer popup (toolbar icon) and make sure Show PiP button on player is enabled. The button appears in the top-right corner of the video on hover.

How do I turn features off?

Click the OnePlayer icon in the Safari toolbar. Each feature — Auto-Pause, Sticky PiP, and the PiP button — has its own toggle.

Does OnePlayer work on anything other than YouTube?

No. OnePlayer is YouTube-only by design and only runs on pages under youtube.com.

Does OnePlayer collect any data?

No. See the Privacy Policy for details. Your settings are stored only on your Mac.

When reporting a bug

To help us fix it quickly, please include:

  • macOS version and Safari version
  • OnePlayer version (visible on the App Store page)
  • A short description of what you did and what happened
  • A YouTube URL where the issue reproduces, if applicable
